## Overview

Pattern selection isn't arbitrary—it's matching the psychological sequence to the audience's current state and defensiveness level.

---

## DECISION TREE START

```
START: What's your audience's relationship to the topic?
│
├─ Skeptical, resistant, allergic to confrontation
│  └─→ SOFT INFILTRATION (4→2→1→3→5→6→7)
│
├─ Self-aware, growth-minded, ready for personal work
│  └─→ IDENTITY FIRST (1→5→2→3→4→6→7)
│
├─ Systems thinkers, executives, analytically minded
│  └─→ SYSTEMIC TRAP (2→1→3→4→5→6→7) ← K2M DEFAULT
│
├─ Value objectivity, essays, building case before personal
│  └─→ LATE IDENTITY (1→2→3→4→5→6→7, delay 5)
│
└─ Mature audiences, ethical topics, responsibility is the point
   └─→ AGENCY WEIGHT (strong 6, extended 7)
```

## DETAILED PATTERN SELECTION GUIDE

### SOFT INFILTRATION (4→2→1→3→5→6→7)

**Select This Pattern When:**

**Audience Characteristics:**
- Thoughtful, philosophical readers
- Academics or researchers
- Readers allergic to confrontation
- Skeptical of direct challenges
- Value intellectual depth over directness

**Content Characteristics:**
- Philosophical or abstract topics
- Pillar 4 content (Business Consciousness)
- Long-form essays (3,000+ words)
- Topics requiring gentle entry

**Success Indicators:**
- Reader feels "this is interesting" before realizing impact
- Gradual build to awareness
- "Wait, this reframes everything" delayed realization

**When NOT to Use:**
- Quick-hit content or tactical advice
- Time-poor audiences wanting directness
- Topics requiring immediate urgency
- How-to or instructional content

---

### IDENTITY FIRST (1→5→2→3→4→6→7)

**Select This Pattern When:**

**Audience Characteristics:**
- Self-aware, growth-minded individuals
- Coaches, founders, creators
- Readers already open to self-examination
- Personal development community
- Ready for personal challenges

**Content Characteristics:**
- Pillar 1 content (Human OS Development)
- Individual agency narratives
- Personal growth topics
- Behavior change focus

**Success Indicators:**
- Immediate "this is about me" recognition
- Uncomfortable but compelling reading
- Hard to disengage once started

**When NOT to Use:**
- Cold/analytical audiences
- First-time readers (no trust established)
- Policy or organizational design topics
- Readers easily triggered by personal pressure

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Build credibility early before Module 5
- Use sparingly to avoid fatigue
- Pair with warm, empathetic tone

---

### SYSTEMIC TRAP (2→1→3→4→5→6→7) ← **K2M DEFAULT**

**Select This Pattern When:**

**Audience Characteristics:**
- Systems thinkers
- C-suite executives
- Consultants, strategy leaders
- Analytically minded professionals
- Policy and organizational design leaders

**Content Characteristics:**
- Pillar 2 content (AI Mapping & Integration)
- Business/organizational topics
- Structural or systemic issues
- Ethically serious topics

**Success Indicators:**
- Reader feels "cold, inevitable" logic
- Difficult to argue with conclusions
- "I never saw this system I'm inside" realization

**When NOT to Use:**
- Already defensive audiences
- Topics requiring emotional warmth
- Personal development narratives
- Readers who intellectualize to escape identity pressure

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Strengthen Module 5 identity pressure
- Use specific examples to prevent abstraction
- Ensure "you are inside this system" lands personally

---

### LATE IDENTITY REVEAL (1→2→3→4→5→6→7 with delayed 5)

**Select This Pattern When:**

**Audience Characteristics:**
- Values objectivity and analysis
- Skeptical readers needing proof first
- Analytical types
- Essays and long-form readers
- Want to observe before being seen

**Content Characteristics:**
- Essays and long-form writing
- Complex topics requiring setup
- Research-heavy pieces
- Framework introductions

**Success Indicators:**
- Reader believes they're observing ideas
- Then realizes "this is actually about me"
- Safe... until it isn't feeling
- Devastating when timed correctly

**When NOT to Use:**
- Personal development audiences (wants directness)
- Quick-hits or short content
- Topics where personal relevance must be obvious
- Readers impatient for practical application

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Signal personal relevance early ("you'll notice this in...")
- Build strong case before identity reveal
- Don't delay too long or connection weakens

---

### AGENCY WEIGHT (Strong 6, Extended 7)

**Select This Pattern When:**

**Audience Characteristics:**
- Mature audiences
- Senior leaders
- Ethics-focused professionals
- Readers making consequential decisions
- Comfortable with weight and responsibility

**Content Characteristics:**
- Ethical or existential topics
- Content meant to echo for days
- Responsibility is the core point
- Heavy topics requiring gravity

**Success Indicators:**
- Heavy, quiet, lingering tone
- Weight lands and stays
- Responsibility feels unavoidable
- Extended open loop creates lasting after-effect

**When NOT to Use:**
- Light topics or how-to content
- Audiences seeking quick wins
- Tactical or instructional content
- Readers already overwhelmed

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Reserve for topics with genuine ethical stakes
- Balance heaviness with clarity
- Don't overuse or loses impact

## AUDIENCE STATE ASSESSMENT

Before selecting pattern, assess audience on three dimensions:

### 1. Defensiveness Level

| Level | Pattern Recommendation |
|-------|----------------------|
| **High defensiveness** | Soft Infiltration (gentle entry) |
| **Medium defensiveness** | Systemic Trap or Late Identity |
| **Low defensiveness** | Identity First (direct challenge) |
| **Open to weight** | Agency Weight |

### 2. Relationship to Topic

| Relationship | Pattern Recommendation |
|-------------|----------------------|
| **Skeptical/unaware** | Soft Infiltration |
| **Problem-aware but stuck** | Systemic Trap |
| **Ready for personal work** | Identity First |
| **Analytical, wants proof** | Late Identity |
| **Already committed, needs weight** | Agency Weight |

### 3. Content Type

| Content | Pattern Recommendation |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Philosophical/Essay** | Soft Infiltration or Late Identity |
| **Personal Development** | Identity First |
| **Business/AI Systems** | Systemic Trap (default) |
| **Ethical/Existential** | Agency Weight |
| **Research/Analysis** | Late Identity |

## PATTERN COMPARISON MATRIX

| Dimension | Soft Infiltration | Identity First | Systemic Trap | Late Identity | Agency Weight |
|-----------|------------------|---------------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Emotional intensity** | Low (builds slowly) | High (immediate) | Medium (cold) | Medium→High (delayed) | High (heavy) |
| **Defensiveness risk** | Very Low | High | Medium | Low | Medium |
| **Build time required** | Long (slow warm) | Short (fast hit) | Medium | Long (patience) | Medium |
| **Best for word count** | 3,000+ | 1,500-2,000 | 2,000-2,500 | 2,500-4,000 | 2,000-3,000 |
| **Tone pairing** | Philosophical | Warm-but-challenging | Cold-inevitable | Direct | Cold-inevitable |
| **CTA placement** | After 5, before 6 | After 5 (early), before 2 | After 5, before 6 | After delayed 5, before 6 | After 5, before strong 6 |
