## The Core Principle

**Subscribe CTA placement is psychologically strategic, not arbitrary.**

The button appears when the reader has experienced identity pressure but has not yet had agency returned. This is **maximum receptivity** — they feel the weight but haven't yet externalized responsibility.

---

## THE GOLDEN RULE

**Place Subscribe CTA: AFTER Module 5 (Identity), BEFORE Module 6 (Agency)**

```
[Module 5: Identity Pressure]
     ↓
  Reader feels: "This describes me"
  Reader hasn't yet: Decided what to do with it
     ↓
[SUBSCRIBE CTA: Maximum Receptivity]
     ↓
[Module 6: Agency Return]
     ↓
  Reader feels: "This is my responsibility"
  Reader may externalize: Escape through "interesting"
```

---

## WHY THIS PLACEMENT WORKS

### Psychological Mechanism

1. **After Module 5 (Identity):**
   - Reader recognizes themselves in the pattern
   - Current behavior conflicts with aspired identity
   - Internal tension created
   - **They want more of this clarity**

2. **Before Module 6 (Agency):**
   - Reader hasn't yet been handed responsibility
   - Weight is palpable but not yet assigned
   - No escape hatch available yet
   - **Subscribe = join the journey, not escape the weight**

3. **Maximum Receptivity Point:**
   - Identity has been shifted (I see this now)
   - Responsibility hasn't been returned (I don't have to act yet)
   - Subscribe = commit to continued clarity, not commit to difficult action
   - **Joining feels like relief, not demand**

## PATTERN-SPECIFIC CTA PLACEMENT

Different patterns shift the CTA timing based on module order:

| Pattern | Module Order | CTA Position | Rationale |
|---------|--------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Systemic Trap** | 2→1→3→4→5→6→7 | After 5, before 6 | Standard placement |
| **Identity First** | 1→5→2→3→4→6→7 | After 5 (early), before 2 | Identity comes early; capture while hot |
| **Soft Infiltration** | 4→2→1→3→5→6→7 | After 5, before 6 | Standard — but reader was warmed slowly |
| **Late Identity** | 1→2→3→4→(delay 5)→6→7 | After delayed 5, before 6 | Wait for the late identity hit |
| **Agency Weight** | Strong 6, extended 7 | After 5, before strong 6 | Don't wait for heavy agency section |

## CTA PLACEMENT ARCHITECT DECISION

When Architect Agent determines CTA placement, consider:

### 1. Pattern-Based Timing

```
IF pattern = "Identity First"
THEN CTA after Module 5 (early capture)

ELSE IF pattern = "Late Identity Reveal"
THEN CTA after delayed Module 5 (wait for reveal)

ELSE
THEN CTA after Module 5, before Module 6 (standard)
```

### 2. Section Structure Placement

In practice, CTA appears mid-section (usually Part IV):

```
## PART IV: [TITLE]

[Content executing Module 5: Identity]

---

[SUBSCRIBE CTA: Place subscription button here]
*Rationale: Reader has experienced identity shift but hasn't yet had agency returned.
Maximum receptivity to joining the journey.*

---

[Content executing Module 6: Agency]
```

## CTA COPY & CONTEXT

### What the CTA Should Say

**Effective K2M CTA Options:**

1. **Join the journey:** "Continue exploring consciousness-first AI integration"
2. **Identity-affirming:** "If you see this pattern, subscribe for deeper clarity"
3. **No-promise:** "Join [X] readers thinking alongside intelligent systems"

**What to Avoid:**

- ❌ "Get weekly tips" (too tactical)
- ❌ "Learn how to..." (implies rescue/solutions)
- ❌ "Don't miss an issue" (fear-based, emotional bullying)
- ❌ Action-oriented CTAs before agency return

### Rationale to Designer

When inserting CTA marker, include context for designer:

```markdown
[SUBSCRIBE CTA: Place subscription button here]
*Rationale: Reader has experienced identity shift but hasn't yet had agency returned.
Maximum receptivity to joining the journey. They feel the weight but want more clarity
before deciding what to do with it.*
```

## COMMON MISTAKES

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| **CTA too early** (before identity) | No personal connection yet | Move after Module 5 |
| **CTA too late** (after agency) | Reader escapes through externalization | Move before Module 6 |
| **CTA at very end** | Closure kills after-effect | Move mid-piece, after identity |
| **Multiple CTAs** | Breaks psychological flow | One strategic placement |
| **CTA with rescue language** | Undermines agency module | Use journey language, not "how-to" |
